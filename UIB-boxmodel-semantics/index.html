Federer withdraws from ATP Cup

Roger Federer will not take part in January's inaugural ATP Cup,
which was set to be a key part of his preparations for the Australian Open.

  • The event, which will take place in Sydney, Brisbane and Perth, will pit national teams against one another, with Federer set to represent Switzerland.
  • However, the competition has now announced that the 20-time Grand Slam champion will no longer take part in the competition, citing family reasons.
  • The 38-year-old's withdrawal also means this his country's qualification will be rescinded.
  • Federer's arch rivals Novak Djokovic and Rafael Nadal are set to represent Serbia and Spain respectively.
Source: Roger Federer

Magical Messi stars as Barca thrash Valladolid to go top

Lionel Messi inspired Barcelona to a 5-1 win over Real Valladolid, scoring twice and assisting two more as the defending champions went top of La Liga. It took the Catalans just three minutes to open the scoring when Clement Lenglet lashed home a left-footed strike from 10 yards out after Real Valladolid failed to clear a Messi freekick into the area. However, the visitors struck back when Kiko Olivas bundled home after a cross had bounced off Marc-Andre Ter Stegen. Barca regained control just before the half hour mark, though, with Messi clipping a pass over the top for Arturo Vidal to divert home. And the 32-year-old provided the highlight of the night moments later, finding the top corner of the net with a trademark freekick.
